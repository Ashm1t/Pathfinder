Brief Facts:
The complainant, Neha Kapoor, states that in April 2026 she was added to a
WhatsApp group named "SmartWealth Traders" by an unknown person claiming to
represent a SEBI-registered trading platform called "SmartWealth App". Over
several weeks she was persuaded to deposit money into four different bank
accounts for "guaranteed returns" on stock trading. She transferred a total
of Rs. 18,40,000 (Eighteen Lakh Forty Thousand only) in six instalments
between 15.04.2026 and 28.05.2026. When she tried to withdraw her profits on
28.05.2026, the app stopped responding and the group admin was no longer
reachable. On verification she found the "SmartWealth App" was not
registered with SEBI and the bank accounts belonged to unrelated persons.

Investigation identified the mastermind as Rohit Bansal, who operated the
fake trading platform and WhatsApp group under a fictitious identity. Sunita
Kadam allowed her bank account to be used to receive and layer the fraud
proceeds (mule account operator). Deepak Wahi recruited victims through
social media and shared referral links.

Sections applied: Section 318 BNS (cheating), Section 316 BNS (criminal
breach of trust), Section 61 BNS (criminal conspiracy), Section 3(5) BNS
(acts done by several persons in furtherance of common intention), Section
66D IT Act (cheating by personation using a computer resource), Section 66C
IT Act (identity theft).

Investigating Officer: Inspector Vikram Solanki, PS Cyber Crime, Gurugram.

Case Status: Under investigation. Three accused arrested. Chargesheet under
preparation.
